About us

Explore our website to learn more about our organization and its beliefs, mission, history, and team to make informed decisions and establish meaningful connections.

Our History

RUMblebots was founded in 1994. This special project aims to create an environment in which every student can grow academically, mentally, and professionally, as well as to aid in the professional development of our members.

Our Mision

To complete combat robot development while allowing its members to learn and apply engineering knowledge and concepts in a real-world, hands-on setting.

Our Vision

RUMblebots' vision is to bring combat robotics to Puerto Rico and host a competition at the University of Puerto Rico, Mayaguez Campus.

Welcome to RUMblebots!

We are a combat robotics club from the University of Puerto Rico, Mayaguez. Our club is a fun and exciting sport that puts robots against one another in a supremacy fight. If you are interested in design and manufacturing, electronics, programming, marketing, and logistics, this club is the place to put your skills and creativity to the test. Members of our club can design, build, and compete with their own robots in a high-energy, adrenaline-fueled environment. So, if you're ready to join the ranks of combat robot enthusiasts and face off against opponents in the arena, come join us and let's rumble!

Through the years

2015
National Competition

Juracan and Cibucan 2.0, two bots from our team, competed in the National Competition. Although both robots were drummers, their missions were distinct. Juracan's weapon proved to be extremely lethal.

2016
First Draft

Cibucan 2.0 was sent out to compete once more. During the competition's optimizing process. Due to time constraints, the design was abandoned for the 2016 competition and repurposed for future bots. This was the initial rough draft of Guarionex's chassis.

2017
Hurricane Maria

After the hit of the Category 5 Hurricane we were unable to compete due to the natural catastrophe. However we decided to focus to raise money for next year to compete once more and represent the puertorrican spirit of moving forward.

2018
National Robotics League

As adaptable and durable as the robot is, and as well-planned and practiced as the strategy is, the most crucial part of the tournament is teamwork. Our team would not have gotten to where they are today without it. RUMblebots is pleased to offer our culture and work ethic via Guarionex, and we intend to win!

2019
Guarionex

The design is built on four principles that became the design's cornerstones. These concepts arose from our team's previous NRL competition. These basic ideas were to integrate the designs of Cibucan 2.0 and Juracan, to build a chassis with interlocking pieces, to improve the bot's balance without adding more wheels, and to build a bot with a longer weapon reach.

2020
Covid-19

It wasn't until February 2020 that Puereto Rico was in quarantine. Although we could not reunite nor interact with one another physically we did everything virtual. We had our weekly meetings online and continued our projects.

2021
Zumo

RUMblebots set out to design and build a 12-pound fighting robot, but they were unable to access their customary workspace. They learnt about manufacturing design, weight control, and component protection. The most essential lesson learned was to be ready for any threats such as an attack. The team adapted and achieved their goals. RUMblebots intends to keep developing the 12 pound modular fighting robot, increase the weight of a 15 pound robot to 30 pounds, and create a 500 gram autonomous sumo robot.

2022
12 pound combat

The team finished designing and constructing a workable 12-pound modular combat robot. The chassis plates were comprised of a titanium, nylon x, and aluminum composite. The weapon was successfully produced, however there is still potential for development. The team also worked on the design of a switch and electrical components, which resulted in a weight savings of 1.1 pounds due to electrical improvements. Before the end of the semester, the switch will be modified and the motors tested.

Our Journey

The robotics club adventure has been both exciting and informative for its members. The club's primary purpose since its start has been to explore the realm of robotics and to cultivate a passion for science and engineering among its members. The adventure began with foundational robotics workshops in which members studied the principles of electronics, programming, and mechanics. Members of the group began working on more complicated projects as the club evolved, such as developing and building autonomous robots capable of executing difficult tasks. The group took part in a variety of competitions and demonstrations, allowing members to showcase their skills and receive exposure to real-world robotics applications.The club has had various problems along the way, such as limited finances and technical difficulties, but with determination, the members have conquered these obstacles and continued to develop and learn. Overall, the robotics club experience has provided members with an excellent opportunity to learn new skills, interact with like-minded individuals, and pursue their enthusiasm for robots.

Our Perception

Over time, people's perceptions of robotics clubs have shifted, with more individuals understanding the relevance of robotics in molding the future. Initially, robotics clubs were thought to be specialized groups for people with a special interest in science and engineering. However, as robots become more prevalent in everyday life, more individuals are beginning to recognize the value of robotics clubs in improving technical skills and promoting creativity. Robotics clubs are increasingly regarded as a significant resource for students interested in learning about electronics, programming, and mechanics while also developing problem-solving and collaborative skills.Robotics clubs have evolved from an exclusive organization for tech aficionados to an inclusive society that welcomes everyone with an interest in robotics to join and learn. As a result, robotics clubs have become an essential component of education, encouraging the next generation of innovators and problem solvers.
